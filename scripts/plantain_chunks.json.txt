Neither P. major nor P. lanceolata are typically classified as invasive in a regulatory sense – they tend to occupy disturbed soil niches and rarely penetrate intact wild ecosystems. However, their persistent seeds and hardy nature mean they can naturalize aggressively in gardens and lawns. Both tolerate a wide pH range, though broadleaf plantain is noted as an indicator of alkaline, compact, moist soils."
